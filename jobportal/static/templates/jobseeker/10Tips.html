10 Resume Tips to Stand Out

Expert advice to help you craft a resume that catches the eye of hiring managers.

1. Tailor Your Resume for Each Job

Customize your resume for each job application by highlighting the skills and experiences most relevant to the position. Use keywords from the job description to make your resume stand out to applicant tracking systems (ATS).

2. Use a Clean and Professional Format

Choose a clean, easy-to-read format with consistent fonts, headings, and bullet points. Avoid clutter and ensure your resume is well-organized.

3. Start with a Strong Summary

Begin your resume with a compelling summary that highlights your key qualifications, achievements, and career goals. Keep it concise and impactful.

4. Quantify Your Achievements

Use numbers and metrics to demonstrate your accomplishments. For example, "Increased sales by 20% in six months" is more impactful than "Improved sales."

5. Focus on Relevant Experience

Highlight your most relevant work experience and achievements. If you have extensive experience, focus on the last 10-15 years and keep older roles brief.

6. Include Keywords from the Job Description

Many companies use ATS to screen resumes. Incorporate keywords and phrases from the job description to increase your chances of passing the initial screening.

7. Keep It Concise

Aim for a one-page resume if you have less than 10 years of experience. For more experienced professionals, two pages are acceptable, but avoid unnecessary details.

8. Use Action Verbs

Start each bullet point with a strong action verb, such as "managed," "developed," "led," or "implemented." This makes your resume more dynamic and engaging.

9. Proofread for Errors

Typos and grammatical errors can make a bad impression. Proofread your resume carefully and consider asking a friend or mentor to review it.

10. Include a Call to Action

End your resume with a call to action, such as "Looking forward to discussing how I can contribute to your team." This shows enthusiasm and initiative.
